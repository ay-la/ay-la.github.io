## welcome! (under construction)

I'm a PhD student in the Department of Linguistics at Yale University, advised by [Dr. Robert Frank](https://bobfrank1.github.io/). 
My primary interests are rooted in computational linguistics. I work with the [Computational Linguistics at Yale](http://clay.yale.edu/) lab.
With the advent of extremely large machine learning models and the vast expanse of text data available online, modern natural language processing techniques have come to yield truly impressive results within a relatively short amount of time. 
My research is vested in exploring what we can do, if anything, to significantly reduce the amount of resources needed for machines to effectively interpret natural language data. As it stands, the requirement of large datasets is not sustainable for extending NLP towards the vast majority of the world's languages.
